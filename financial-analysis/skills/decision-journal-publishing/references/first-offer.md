# First Offer

## Recommendation

Launch one paid offer, not two.

### Offer 0: Free lead magnet

`Lite 事件阅读模板`

### Offer 1: First paid product

`事件驱动研判模板包 v1`

This keeps the first month focused on one real commercial question:

`有没有人愿意为可复用判断模板付费？`

## Offer 0: Lite Event-Reading Template

### Role

Lead magnet for both professional users and high-participation retail users.

### Price

- free

### Promise

Give a minimal structure for reading sudden events without turning the account
into a tip service.

### Contents

1. one-page reading skeleton
2. confirmed / unconfirmed / inference legend
3. trigger and invalidation checklist
4. one worked example
5. one CTA to the full template pack

### What It Is Not

1. not a paid starter product
2. not a target-price product
3. not an individualized portfolio recommendation

## Offer 1: Event-Driven Template Pack v1

### Role

Core paid asset for professionals and serious self-directed investors.

### Price

- launch price: RMB 199-299
- normal price after validation: RMB 299-399

### Target Use Cases

1. policy shocks
2. geopolitical headlines
3. earnings and company-event surprises
4. sudden sector narrative changes

### Pack Contents

1. event-driven main template
2. evidence classification rules
3. conflict matrix
4. scenario tree
5. trigger board
6. invalidation checklist
7. one-page briefing sheet
8. three completed sample cases
9. one AI-assisted workflow
10. version log

### Why This Should Go First

1. it fits the existing `news-index -> article-workflow` engine
2. it serves both pro and advanced retail users without collapsing into hype
3. it creates a clean bridge into later membership

## Deferred Offer

Do not launch this in the first 30 days:

`事件阅读卡 / 风险拆解卡`

Only test it later if:

1. the template pack already sells
2. retail demand is strong but too price-sensitive
3. users ask for a simpler reading aid, not for stock tips

## Membership Upgrade Path

Membership should sell:

1. template updates
2. sample-library growth
3. workflow upgrades
4. weekly review pack
5. scorekeeping archive

Do not sell membership as "more content". Sell it as "judgment assets that keep
improving".

## Launch Sequence

### Week 1

1. publish two to three free posts using the same judgment structure
2. launch the lite template lead magnet
3. collect wording friction from both pro and retail readers

### Week 2

1. publish one worked example and one anti-hype explainer
2. open a waitlist or interest collection for the template pack
3. refine the sales page from reader questions

### Week 3

1. launch template pack v1
2. publish one worked example with CTA
3. separate pack buyers from casual subscribers

### Week 4

1. gather feedback from buyers
2. publish one update note or usage proof
3. decide whether the pack deserves a v1.1

## Decision Test

The first offer is working if:

1. the lead magnet captures identifiable demand
2. the core pack converts a smaller but higher-quality buyer group
3. buyer feedback focuses on field usability, not hype requests
