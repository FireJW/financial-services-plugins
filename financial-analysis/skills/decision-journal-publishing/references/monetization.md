# Monetization

## User Types By Payment Logic

### 1. High-frequency decision users

They pay to reduce repeat decision cost.

They buy:

1. speed
2. structure
3. repeatability
4. lower error cost

### 2. Low-frequency, high-stakes users

They pay when one decision matters a lot.

They buy:

1. confidence framing
2. board-ready outputs
3. custom support

Use them as selective upside, not the main engine.

### 3. Output externalizers

They need to turn information into materials for other people.

They buy:

1. templates
2. workflows
3. presentation-ready structures

## Free vs Paid Boundary

Free gives:

1. what happened
2. why it matters
3. what is confirmed vs unconfirmed
4. three key variables to watch
5. a simple risk boundary

Paid gives:

1. reusable template
2. scoring logic
3. trigger rules
4. workflow maps
5. sample library
6. update rights

Do not make paid content just a longer free article.

## Product Ladder

### Level 0: Free

- evidence-layered posts
- quick cards
- scoreboards
- lite template lead magnet

### Level 1: First paid product

Event-driven template pack.

Suggested range:

- RMB 199-399

Use a launch price only if it helps close the first real loop. Do not position the
core product like a cheap information good.

### Level 2: Recurring membership

Launch only after users prove they want updates, not just a one-time file.

Includes:

1. all template updates
2. new sample cases
3. workflow upgrades
4. monthly review pack
5. scorekeeping archive

Suggested range:

- RMB 1299-2499 per year

### Level 3: Selective high-ticket

- custom packs
- advisory retainers
- workshop or consulting

Do not rely on this level to validate the model.

## First-Month Rule

In the first 30 days:

1. sell one paid product
2. use one lead magnet
3. defer standalone low-ticket paid products
4. defer membership launch

The goal is to validate buyer intent, not maximize ladder complexity.

## Best First Product

Start with:

`事件驱动研判模板包 v1`

Include:

1. main judgment template
2. evidence classification rules
3. conflict matrix template
4. scenario tree template
5. one-page briefing template
6. three finished examples
7. one AI workflow

## Funnel Split

### Pro path

1. WeChat post
2. lite template
3. template pack
4. later membership

### Retail path

1. Toutiao or Xiaohongshu wrapper
2. lite event-reading sheet
3. same template pack with simpler landing-page copy
4. later optional retail add-on only if demand is strong

Retail should broaden acquisition, not change the core product.

## Later Retail Add-On

Only after the template pack sells consistently can you test:

1. `事件阅读卡`
2. a simplified retail wrapper
3. a low-ticket add-on in the RMB 29-59 range

Do not launch this first.

## Metrics That Matter

1. lite-template capture rate
2. template-pack conversion rate
3. pro-lead ratio vs generic retail leads
4. refund rate
5. repeat purchase or update interest rate
6. subscriber-to-buyer quality by platform

## Benchmarking Rule For Monetization

When reviewing high-read articles, always score them on two separate axes:

1. `distribution success`
2. `commercial usefulness`

A case belongs in your monetization benchmark set only if it teaches at least one
of these:

1. how free content hands off to a paid asset
2. how the paid layer reduces time or error cost
3. how the audience signals repeat demand

Do not let pure read volume overrule buyer quality.

## Stop Signals

1. traffic rises but template sales stay flat
2. buyers say "too broad" or "not usable"
3. retail growth forces the tone toward certainty theatre
4. membership does not sell because updates do not feel essential
