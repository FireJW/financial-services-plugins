# Founder / CEO Review

## Product Reframe

This is not yet an "auto-publishing system."

What we really have today is a strong article-production core that can turn a topic into a reviewable WeChat draft package. That is useful. It is also narrower, and clearer, than the story we have sometimes told ourselves.

The right product framing is:

**Find a topic worth writing, write a strong article fast, then let a human publish with confidence.**

## What Is Working

The middle of the workflow is the best part.

- The writing flow is evidence-first, not pure vibes.
- The system can move from topic to brief to draft to revision in a structured way.
- The outputs are tangible, reviewable, and reusable, not just a chat answer.
- The WeChat export package is real product progress because it gives the operator something they can inspect and use.

This matters because the hardest part of the product is not generating words. It is generating something a human would actually trust enough to send.

## What Is Weak

The product is fuzzy at the front and shaky at the end.

- Topic discovery is still narrow and brittle. It can find things, but not yet with the reliability of a product someone would leave running.
- The publish step is too ambiguous. "Draft package" and "ready to publish" are not the same thing.
- Human review is not the star of the last mile yet, even though it should be.
- The system story is confusing. A user can easily think this is already a full WeChat publishing machine. It is not.

That creates the worst kind of product risk: good internals, blurry promise.

## The Right 3-Stage End-State

### Stage 1: Find

Build a dependable topic desk.

The job here is simple: surface a small number of timely, high-value topics with enough source quality to justify writing. Not "show me noise." Show me what is worth spending attention on.

Success looks like:

- reliable topic intake
- clear reason why this topic won
- obvious source quality and freshness

### Stage 2: Write

Turn evidence into a draft a serious operator can work with.

This is already the strongest layer. Keep the evidence firewall. Keep the staged flow. Keep the ability to revise instead of regenerating from scratch.

Success looks like:

- clear brief before prose
- draft that stays inside evidence boundaries
- revision flow that actually learns from human feedback

### Stage 3: Publish

Make publishing calm, explicit, and safe.

This should not feel like "pray and click." It should feel like a publishing cockpit: final review, status truth, asset checks, one clear push action, and a visible record of what happened.

Success looks like:

- human approval is explicit
- publish status is truthful
- failures are easy to understand
- a human can push with confidence

## Top Priorities

1. Clarify the product promise.

Stop selling this internally as full auto-publish. The honest promise is: find, write, prepare, then publish with human control.

2. Make the publish step trustworthy.

The last step needs clear review gates and true status reporting. If the publish layer feels unsafe, the whole product feels unsafe.

3. Upgrade topic discovery from demo to desk.

The system needs better source breadth, better ranking, and more confidence in why a topic deserves an article.

4. Turn human review into a product feature, not a workaround.

The human is not a patch for weak automation. The human is the editor. Productize that role.

## Bottom-Line Judgment

Keep the article core. Tighten the product story. Fix the last mile. Then improve the first mile.

That is the clean path from "promising workflow" to "real publishing product."
