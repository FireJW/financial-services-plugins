# XLK-XLE Monthly One Sheet

As of: 2026-03-27

Use this sheet once per month. The goal is not to predict every move. The goal
is to decide whether the `XLK + XLE` combo still matches the macro regime.

## 1. Regime Check

| Signal | Up / Stable / Down | What it means |
|--------|---------------------|---------------|
| US manufacturing PMI |  | Above 50 and improving means growth is still alive |
| US unemployment rate 3m trend |  | A clear uptrend means recession risk is rising |
| Core inflation 3m annualized |  | Re-acceleration means inflation pressure is returning |
| 10Y real yield |  | Higher real yields pressure XLK valuation |
| Brent 1-3m trend |  | A steady rise supports XLE if demand has not broken |
| XLE vs Brent |  | If XLE lags Brent, energy equity is not confirming the oil move |
| HY credit spread / stress proxy |  | Wider spreads mean late-cycle or recession risk |

## 2. Regime Classification

| Regime | Typical pattern | Default stance |
|--------|-----------------|----------------|
| Soft landing | PMI stable or improving, inflation easing, real yields stable/down | `60% XLK + 40% XLE` |
| Re-inflation | PMI not weak, inflation re-accelerating, Brent firm, XLE confirming | `50% XLK + 50% XLE` |
| Early stagflation | PMI softening, inflation sticky/up, Brent up, XLE still confirming | `50% XLK + 50% XLE` |
| Late stagflation | PMI below 50, unemployment rising, credit stress up, XLE stops confirming | `30% XLK + 30% XLE + 40% short bonds/cash` |
| Recession | Growth weak, unemployment rising, spreads widening, Brent weak | `30% XLK + 30% XLE + 40% short bonds/cash` or more defensive |

## 3. Position Template

| Template | Use when | Main risk |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| `50% XLK + 50% XLE` | Growth and inflation may rotate; next leader is not obvious | Not a low-drawdown portfolio |
| `60% XLK + 40% XLE` | Soft landing and AI capex remain the main driver | Real yields rise again and compress XLK |
| `30% XLK + 30% XLE + 40% short bonds/cash` | Late stagflation or recession risk is rising | Underperforms if risk appetite re-expands quickly |

## 4. Rebalance Rules

| Rule | Trigger |
|------|---------|
| Review frequency | Once per month |
| Hard rebalance | Every 6-12 months |
| Drift trigger | Any sleeve moves more than 8 percentage points from target |
| Template switch | Only switch after 2 consecutive month-end checks confirm the new regime |
| Cash usage | Use new cash/dividends to top up the underweight sleeve first |

## 5. Failure Signals

| Failure signal | Why it matters | Action |
|---------------|----------------|--------|
| 10Y real yield keeps rising and XLK earnings are not accelerating | XLK loses valuation support | Cut XLK weight |
| Brent stays strong but XLE underperforms Brent | Energy equity is not validating the oil thesis | Cut XLE weight |
| PMI falls below 50 and keeps weakening while unemployment rises | The regime is shifting toward late stagflation or recession | Reduce total beta |
| Core inflation stays sticky while credit spreads widen | Worst mix: high inflation plus weak growth | Move to defensive template |
